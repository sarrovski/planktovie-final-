About Planktovie

Founded in 2016 in Marseille, France — advancing aquatic organism nutrition through rigorous science.

Our Story

Modern aquariology techniques have made it possible to maintain and reproduce species of corals and fish that previously could not survive outside their natural habitats. Simultaneously, biomedical research has increasingly turned to aquatic organisms — the zebrafish (Danio rerio) is now used in nearly every research center worldwide to study cancer and other diseases.

Despite this growing demand, nutritional solutions meeting the very specific needs of captive-bred aquatic organisms were severely lacking. Planktovie was founded to bridge this gap — offering laboratories and the aquarium market research-grade nutrient solutions and precision equipment.

Our Mission

We develop, produce, and distribute nutritional solutions and equipment responding to the highest standards for maintaining aquatic organisms in captivity — serving scientific research, aquaculture, and the aquarium hobby across more than 40 countries.

Our Brand & Technology

Proprietary solutions developed in partnership with CNRS and validated through peer-reviewed research.

FishGun Feed Dispensers

Semi-automatic dispensers designed to improve feeding reproducibility and precision while eliminating musculoskeletal strain for laboratory technicians. The FishGun D2 handles solid feeds across all zebrafish developmental stages. The L3 adds live zooplankton transport capability with RFID-based tank identification.

Growing Micro Feed Lines

A stable extruded feed with neutral buoyancy suitable for all stages of fish development, from larval to adult. Growing Micro Asta+ is enriched with Astaxanthin, strengthening both coloration and immune response in marine fish and invertebrates.

Live Plankton Cultures

We cultivate zooplankton (rotifers, Daphnia, Artemia) fed with carefully selected phytoplankton diets rich in Astaxanthin. All live cultures undergo regular QM Diagnostics sanitary testing to ensure pathogen-free status.

Photo-Bioreactor Technology

In partnership with CNRS, we developed photo-bioreactors for high-yield production of difficult microalgae species such as dinoflagellates — now available to the scientific community worldwide.

Training Programmes

Expert-led courses for laboratory technicians, aquaculturists, and aquarists.

14h · Biology

Rotifer Culture (Brachionus plicatilis)

Theory and practice of saltwater rotifer culture for zebrafish larval feeding — inoculation, feeding regimes, density monitoring, and quality control. Suitable for zootechnicians and lab managers.

14h · Biology

Daphnia Culture (Daphnia magna)

Comprehensive training on Daphnia culture for zebrafish facilities — water quality management, phytoplankton feeding, harvesting, and integration with feeding automation equipment.

14h · Immunology

Theoretical Immunology

Core concepts of cellular and molecular immunology for researchers and technicians working with aquatic models. Covers innate and adaptive immunity, experimental approaches, and interpretation of immune data.

35h · Business

Launching a Biotech Start-up

Everything needed to create a company in life sciences — mixing science, finance, law, market analysis, communication, and regulatory affairs. Taught in French or English, inter- or intra-company format.

7h · Molecular Biology

Quantitative PCR

Practical and theoretical aspects of qPCR for life science researchers — primer design, normalisation, analysis of results, and common pitfalls. Hands-on sessions with real experimental data.

14h · Biostatistics

Computer-Based Biostatistics

Statistical methods for biological data analysis using software tools. Covers experimental design, descriptive statistics, hypothesis testing, and visualisation of results for scientific publications.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How should live rotifers be stored upon delivery? +
Store at room temperature (20–25°C) and use within the specified shelf life printed on the container. Gently swirl before use to ensure even distribution of organisms.
What is the shelf life of Growing Micro? +
12 months when stored in a cool, dry environment. Once opened, use within 3 months and keep the container sealed with included desiccant bags.
Do you ship internationally? +
Yes, we ship to over 40 countries. Live products require special shipping arrangements — please contact us for international shipping quotes and estimated timelines.
Is institutional or volume pricing available? +
Yes. We offer dedicated pricing for research institutions, universities, and bulk orders. Please submit a Request Quote form and our team will respond within 24 hours.
What certifications do your products carry? +
All live cultures undergo regular QM Diagnostics sanitary testing. Our protocols are developed in partnership with CNRS and validated through peer-reviewed scientific publications.
What maintenance does the FishGun require? +
The FishGun requires cleaning after each use cycle as described in the included manual. We offer maintenance service contracts and on-site training programmes.
How do I culture rotifers for zebrafish larvae? +
Prepare 50L of culture water at 15 ppt salinity. Inoculate with 5–10 million rotifers from a commercial supplier. Feed daily with Phyto-Feast or RGcomplete phytoplankton. Maintain at 20–25°C with gentle aeration. Harvest after 48–72h when density reaches 200–400 rotifers/mL. See our full rotifer culture protocol in the Resources section.
What is the recommended feeding regimen for adult zebrafish? +
Adult zebrafish (Danio rerio) typically require 30 mg of Gemma Micro 500 per individual per day. When using the FishGun D2, set vibration time according to the calibration table in the user guide. Supplement with live Daphnia or rotifers 2–3 times per week for optimal reproduction rates.
How long does Daphnia take to culture? +
Daphnia magna cultures typically establish within 7–10 days from inoculation. Feed daily with green water phytoplankton or Phyto-Feast. Maintain water temperature at 20–22°C. Daphnia is particularly valuable for feeding zebrafish from 60 days post-fertilisation through adulthood.
Can training be reimbursed through professional training funds? +
Yes. Planktovie is Qualiopi-certified, making all training programmes eligible for reimbursement through your company's OPCO training fund or the FNE device. Contact formation@planktovie.biz for a formal quote and the required documentation.
Who are your distribution partners in Europe? +
Planktovie distributes through a network of regional specialists: Plexx B.V. (Netherlands & Germany), Histoire d'eaux (France), Marine Home (France), Seafoodia, and Sodispan. Contact us to find the nearest distributor in your country.
